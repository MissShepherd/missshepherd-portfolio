Quest Log
01

The route was not straight.

My path into software development included restarts, changes in direction, and experience outside the usual tech bubble. It made me adaptable, taught me how to begin again, and proved that taking longer does not mean standing still.

02

I learned by building real things.

Practical projects taught me how much work lives between an idea and a finished interface: planning components, connecting APIs, handling loading and error states, writing tests, fixing bugs, and making the whole application survive outside the happy path.

03

Creativity needs structure.

I like loud colours, unusual layouts, textured interfaces, and details with character. But personality should support the experience rather than bury it. My goal is to make expressive work that remains usable, responsive, and easy to navigate.

04

Still learning. Still building.

I do not treat development like a completed skill tree. Every project adds another tool, exposes another weak spot, and gives me something new to improve. I may overthink the route, but I keep moving and eventually ship the build.
